Research

Hi—thanks for stopping by. I study how to make decisions that hold up when data or environments shift. My work focuses on uncertainty quantification and decision-making under uncertainty, aiming for methods that are principled yet practical. Ongoing work Nonparametric confidence intervals via the Cheap Bootstrap (with Prof. Henry Lam): Asymptotically exact CIs for bagged predictors using a small, fixed number of resamples with growing bag size; applicable to Random Forests and bagged SAA in stochastic optimization. Arindam_CV Superiority of naïve optimization via stochastic dominance (with Prof. Henry Lam): When ERM can outperform DRO/regularization in worst-case regret under joint data and distributional uncertainty. Arindam_CV Rare-event probabilities using Extreme Value Theory (with Prof. Henry Lam): Estimating tail probabilities using GPD as a model-agnostic alternative to importance sampling. Arindam_CV Fantasy-sports user behavior (with Dream Sports): Causal impact of platform actions (promotions/incentives) on engagement at production scale. Arindam_CV Multivariate flight-data modeling (NASA Langley): Tractable optimization with polynomial features; internal Python package and MATLAB implementation; estimation with non-normalized statistical models. Arindam_CV Patents & publications Efficient Uncertainty Quantification of Bagging via the Cheap Bootstrap. Proceedings of the Winter Simulation Conference (WSC) 2025. (with H. Lam). Arindam_CV Methods and systems for developing decision-tree machine-learning models. US 2024/0386287 A1. (Saha, Dasgupta, Tewari, Ramesh, Roy Chowdhury, et al.). Arindam_CV Technical reports & working notes Drafts available on request covering the topics above: Cheap Bootstrap for bagging, ERM vs. DRO under joint uncertainty, EVT-based rare-event estimation, and large-scale behavioral analytics. Arindam_CV Invited talks NASA Langley Research Centre (Nov 2024), Uncertainty Quantification Seminar: “Modeling Multivariate Data using Exponential Polynomials”; “Stochastic Optimization: Superiority of Naïve Approaches.” Arindam_CV INFORMS Annual Meeting (Oct 2024), Recent Advances in Data-Driven Optimization: “Robustness vs Statistical Efficiency: Superiority of Naïve Optimization.” Arindam_CV New England Statistics Symposium (May 2024), Recent Advances in Data-Driven Decision-Making: “Regret Optimality of Empirical Risk Minimization.”
